## What is Stock Analyzer?

Stock Analyzer is a professional stock valuation tool that helps you make informed investment decisions by analyzing fundamental financial metrics. It uses a comprehensive scoring methodology to evaluate whether a stock is undervalued, fairly valued, or overvalued.

### Key Benefits:
- **Objective Analysis**: Remove emotion from investment decisions with data-driven scores
- **Comprehensive Evaluation**: Analyzes 7 key financial metrics with weighted scoring
- **Industry Comparison**: Compare stocks against industry benchmarks
- **AI-Powered Insights**: Get intelligent recommendations and risk assessments
- **PDF Report Upload**: Extract financial data automatically from annual reports

## How to Analyze a Stock

### Method 1: Manual Data Entry

1. **Navigate to Home Page**
   - You'll see the stock input form

2. **Enter Basic Information**
   - **Stock Name**: Company name (e.g., "Apple Inc.")
   - **Ticker Symbol**: Stock symbol (e.g., "AAPL")

3. **Enter Price & Earnings Data**
   - **Current Stock Price**: Latest trading price
   - **EPS (TTM)**: Earnings Per Share - Trailing Twelve Months
   - **EPS (Forward)**: Projected EPS for next year (optional)
   - **EPS Growth %**: Year-over-year earnings growth rate

4. **Enter Financial Metrics**
   - **Revenue**: Total company revenue
   - **Net Profit**: Company's net income
   - **Total Debt**: Outstanding debt obligations
   - **Cash Flow**: Operating cash flow
   - **Book Value Per Share**: Net asset value per share

5. **Enter Market Ratios**
   - **P/E Ratio**: Price-to-Earnings ratio
   - **P/BV Ratio**: Price-to-Book Value ratio
   - **ROE %**: Return on Equity percentage
   - **PEG Ratio**: Price/Earnings to Growth ratio
   - **EV/EBITDA**: Enterprise Value to EBITDA
   - **Dividend Yield %**: Annual dividend as percentage of price

6. **Enter Industry Benchmarks**
   - **Industry Avg P/E**: Average P/E for the sector
   - **Industry Avg P/BV**: Average P/BV for the sector
   - **Industry Avg ROE**: Average ROE for the sector

7. **Click "Analyze Stock"**
   - Results appear instantly

### Method 2: Upload Financial Report (PDF)

1. **Locate the Upload Section**
   - Find "Upload Financial Report" card on home page

2. **Select PDF File**
   - Click "Choose File"
   - Select company's annual report or financial statement (PDF format)

3. **Extract Data**
   - Click "Extract Data"
   - AI processes the PDF (takes 10-30 seconds)
   - Extracted data appears automatically

4. **Review & Adjust**
   - Verify extracted values
   - Fill in any missing fields manually
   - Add industry benchmarks

5. **Analyze**
   - Click "Analyze Stock" to generate results

## Understanding Your Results

### Overall Score (0-5 Scale)

Your analysis generates an overall score based on 7 weighted factors:

| Score Range | Verdict | Recommendation | Meaning |
|-------------|---------|----------------|---------|
| 4.0 - 5.0 | **Undervalued** | Strong Buy | Excellent investment opportunity |
| 3.0 - 3.9 | **Fairly Valued** | Hold | Reasonably priced, suitable for long-term |
| 2.0 - 2.9 | **Overvalued** | Avoid | Risky, wait for better entry point |
| 0.0 - 1.9 | **Overvalued** | Sell | Poor fundamentals, consider exiting |

### Score Card Components

**1. Overall Score Display**
- Large number (0-5) showing composite score
- Color-coded: Green (good), Yellow (neutral), Red (poor)

**2. Verdict**
- Undervalued / Fairly Valued / Overvalued

**3. Recommendation**
- Strong Buy / Buy / Hold / Avoid / Sell

**4. Key Strengths**
- Bullet list of positive factors
- Examples: "Strong ROE of 18.5%", "Attractive P/E ratio"

**5. Key Concerns**
- Bullet list of risk factors
- Examples: "High debt levels", "Weak earnings growth"

### Metrics Grid

Quick view of 4 critical metrics:

**P/E Ratio vs Industry**
- Shows if stock is cheaper or more expensive than peers
- Lower is generally better (but context matters)

**ROE (Return on Equity)**
- Measures profitability efficiency
- Above 15% is excellent, above 20% is exceptional

**EPS Growth**
- Shows earnings momentum
- Above 15% indicates strong growth

**Debt Level**
- Indicates financial risk
- Lower debt-to-equity is safer

### Ratio Comparison Table

Detailed comparison showing:
- **Your Stock**: Actual ratio values
- **Industry Average**: Benchmark comparison
- **Difference**: Percentage above/below industry
- **Status**: Visual indicator (✓ good, ⚠ neutral, ✗ poor)

### Detailed Breakdown

**Quantitative Analysis Tab**
- Shows how each factor contributes to overall score
- Displays individual scores (0-5) for each metric
- Shows weight percentage for each factor
- Calculates weighted contribution

**Scoring Factors & Weights:**
1. **P/E vs Industry** (20%): Valuation relative to peers
2. **P/BV vs Book Value** (15%): Asset-based valuation
3. **ROE Score** (15%): Profitability efficiency
4. **EPS Growth** (20%): Earnings momentum
5. **Debt Score** (10%): Financial stability
6. **Cash Flow Score** (10%): Liquidity strength
7. **Dividend Score** (10%): Income generation

### Visual Charts

**1. Score Breakdown Chart**
- Radar/spider chart showing all 7 factors
- Quickly identify strengths and weaknesses

**2. Ratio Comparison Chart**
- Bar chart comparing your stock vs industry averages
- Easy visual comparison of key ratios

**3. Weight Distribution Chart**
- Pie chart showing how different factors are weighted
- Understand what matters most in the analysis

### AI-Powered Features

**AI Valuation Analysis**
- Intelligent interpretation of your results
- Contextual insights based on all metrics
- Considers market conditions and trends

**Risk Assessment**
- Identifies specific risk factors
- Categorizes risks (financial, operational, market)

**AI Insights**

## Making Investment Decisions

### Step-by-Step Decision Framework

#### 1. Review Overall Score
- **Score 4.0+**: Strong candidate for investment
- **Score 3.0-3.9**: Suitable for conservative portfolios
- **Score <3.0**: Requires caution or avoidance

#### 2. Analyze Strengths vs Concerns
- Count number of strengths vs concerns
- Assess severity of concerns
- Determine if strengths outweigh risks

#### 3. Check Industry Comparison
- Is P/E ratio competitive?
- Is ROE above industry average?
- Are growth rates superior to peers?

#### 4. Evaluate Financial Health
- **Debt Score**: Is debt manageable?
- **Cash Flow**: Is company generating positive cash?
- **Dividend**: Is income sustainable?

#### 5. Consider Growth Potential
- **EPS Growth**: Is company expanding earnings?
- **PEG Ratio**: Is growth priced reasonably? (PEG < 1 is attractive)
- **Revenue Trends**: Is top-line growing?

#### 6. Review AI Insights
- Read AI-generated recommendations
- Consider risk assessment findings
- Evaluate strategic suggestions

### Decision Matrix

| Overall Score | Strengths > Concerns | Industry Comparison | Decision |
|---------------|---------------------|---------------------|----------|
| 4.0+ | Yes | Better than industry | **Strong Buy** - Allocate significant capital |
| 4.0+ | Yes | Similar to industry | **Buy** - Good investment opportunity |
| 3.0-3.9 | Yes | Better than industry | **Buy** - Solid long-term hold |
| 3.0-3.9 | Equal | Similar to industry | **Hold** - Monitor for changes |
| 3.0-3.9 | No | Worse than industry | **Avoid** - Wait for improvement |
| <3.0 | Any | Any | **Avoid/Sell** - High risk |

### Investment Strategies by Score

**Strong Buy (4.0+)**
- Allocate 5-10% of portfolio
- Consider dollar-cost averaging for large positions
- Set target price 20-30% above current
- Hold for 1-3 years minimum

**Buy (3.5-3.9)**
- Allocate 3-5% of portfolio
- Enter gradually over 2-3 months
- Set target price 15-20% above current
- Review quarterly

**Hold (3.0-3.4)**
- Maintain existing position if owned
- Don't add new capital
- Monitor for score improvement
- Review semi-annually

**Avoid (<3.0)**
- Don't initiate new positions
- Consider selling if owned
- Wait for fundamental improvement
- Re-analyze in 6-12 months

## Best Practices

### Data Quality

**Ensure Accurate Inputs**
- Use latest financial statements (10-K, 10-Q)
- Verify data from multiple sources
- Update industry benchmarks regularly
- Use forward EPS when available

**Where to Find Data:**
- Company investor relations websites
- Yahoo Finance, Google Finance
- SEC EDGAR database (for US stocks)
- Financial news platforms (Bloomberg, Reuters)

### Analysis Frequency

**How Often to Analyze:**
- **Quarterly**: After earnings reports
- **Monthly**: For active trading positions
- **Semi-annually**: For long-term holdings
- **Immediately**: After major news events

### Interpretation Tips

**Context Matters**
- High P/E isn't always bad (growth stocks)
- Low P/E isn't always good (declining companies)
- Compare within same industry/sector
- Consider economic cycle stage

**Red Flags to Watch:**
- Declining ROE over multiple quarters
- Negative cash flow despite profits
- Debt-to-equity above 1.0
- EPS growth slowing significantly
- P/E ratio 2x+ industry average

**Green Flags to Seek:**
- Consistent ROE above 15%
- Strong positive cash flow
- Low debt-to-equity (<0.5)
- EPS growth above 15%
- P/E ratio below industry average

### Risk Management

**Diversification**
- Don't invest based on single analysis
- Spread across multiple stocks
- Consider different sectors
- Balance growth and value stocks

**Position Sizing**
- Strong Buy: Max 10% of portfolio
- Buy: Max 5% of portfolio
- Never exceed 15% in single stock

**Stop Losses**
- Set stop loss 15-20% below entry
- Adjust as stock appreciates
- Re-analyze if stop is triggered

### Limitations to Remember

**This Tool Does NOT:**
- Predict short-term price movements
- Account for market sentiment
- Consider macroeconomic factors
- Replace professional financial advice
- Guarantee investment returns

**This Tool DOES:**
- Provide objective fundamental analysis
- Compare stocks to industry benchmarks
- Identify financial strengths and weaknesses
- Offer data-driven recommendations
- Help make informed decisions

## Troubleshooting

### PDF Upload Issues

**Problem**: PDF extraction fails
- **Solution**: Ensure PDF contains text (not scanned images)
- **Solution**: Try a different financial report
- **Solution**: Check OpenAI API key is configured

**Problem**: Missing data after extraction
- **Solution**: Manually fill missing fields
- **Solution**: Use more detailed financial reports
- **Solution**: Verify PDF quality and format

### Analysis Issues

**Problem**: Unrealistic scores
- **Solution**: Verify all input data is accurate
- **Solution**: Check units (millions vs billions)
- **Solution**: Ensure industry benchmarks are correct

**Problem**: Can't save analysis
- **Solution**: Check browser local storage is enabled
- **Solution**: Clear browser cache and retry
- **Solution**: Try different browser
